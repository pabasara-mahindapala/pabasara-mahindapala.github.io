The big tech is having a "Blame Canada" moment.

![](/public/images/blame-canada-moment-of-the-big-tech/guardian-article.png "The Guardian")

Recent lawsuits against Meta and YouTube have highlighted the potential harms of social media, especially for young people. The jury's decision to hold these companies liable for designing addictive products has sparked a broader conversation about the responsibility of tech companies in safeguarding their users' mental health.

*Times have changed, our kids are getting worse*

*They won't obey their parents..*

*Should we blame the government?*

*Or blame society?*

*Or should we blame the images on TV?*

*Blame Canada! Blame Canada!*

*We must blame them and cause a fuss*

*Before somebody thinks of blaming us*

In the 1999 South Park movie, the song "Blame Canada" humorously describes how parents of the children who were addicted to vulgar Canadian media blamed the country for their children's behaviour. They sang *"We must blame them and cause a fuss, before somebody thinks of blaming us"*.

It's true that media and content are designed to be addictive, engaging and appealing for a younger audience. I agree with the verdict that they should be held accountable for the harmful designs of their products.

However, we cannot just blame "Canada" or the tech companies.

If a 3-year-old cannot have their meal without watching Cocomelon, we need to think how they got to that point in the first place.

While we hold the tech companies accountable for their designs, we also need to look at the role of parents, educators, and society in guiding children towards healthier digital habits and media consumption.

There will always be content that is designed to be addictive. 25 years ago, it was the television. Now it is Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok and YouTube. In another 25 years, it will be something else, probably more addictive and immersive.

As a society, we need to take responsibility for our digital habits and consumption. We need to know that digital content will always be designed to be addictive. For children and adults alike, maintaining proper screen time habits will be more important than ever.
